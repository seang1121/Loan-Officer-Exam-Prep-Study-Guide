Underwriting is the heart of the mortgage process ‚Äî it's where loan decisions are made. The SAFE exam tests credit score thresholds, derogatory credit waiting periods, AUS systems, and appraisal independence. Expect 12-18 questions from this area.

### 1. Credit Scores ‚Äî The Basics

**What Is a Credit Score?**
A credit score is a numerical representation of a consumer's creditworthiness based on their credit history. The most commonly used model is the **FICO score** (Fair Isaac Corporation).

**Tri-Merge Credit Report:**
For mortgages, lenders pull credit from all 3 bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) and use the **middle score** of the three for qualifying purposes.

- If there are two borrowers, lenders use the **lower middle score** of the two borrowers
- Example: Borrower 1 scores: 720, 740, 735 ‚Üí middle score = 735
- Borrower 2 scores: 680, 695, 688 ‚Üí middle score = 688
- Qualifying score = **688** (lower of the two middle scores)

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** Always use the **middle score** from the tri-merge report. With multiple borrowers, use the **lower** of the qualifying scores.

---

**FICO Score Ranges:**

| Range | Rating | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **800-850** | Exceptional | Best rates, easiest approvals |
| **740-799** | Very Good | Great rates, minimal scrutiny |
| **670-739** | Good | Standard rates, most programs available |
| **580-669** | Fair | Higher rates, limited programs, FHA possible |
| **Below 580** | Poor | Very limited options |

---

**Minimum Credit Scores by Loan Type:**

| Loan Type | Minimum Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Conventional** | **620** (typically) | Fannie/Freddie guidelines |
| **FHA (3.5% down)** | **580** | With 3.5% minimum down payment |
| **FHA (10% down)** | **500-579** | 10% down payment required |
| **VA** | **No VA minimum** (lenders often require 620) | VA itself doesn't set a score floor |
| **USDA** | **640** (typically) | USDA requires GUS approval |
| **Jumbo** | **700-720+** (varies by lender) | Non-agency, lender sets own rules |

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** FHA has a hard floor of **500** (with 10% down). Below 500 = no FHA loan. The **VA itself does not set a minimum score** ‚Äî this is tested.

---

### 2. Credit Report Components ‚Äî The FICO Formula

FICO scores are calculated using 5 factors:

| Factor | Weight | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Payment History** | **35%** | On-time vs. late payments ‚Äî largest factor |
| **Amounts Owed** | **30%** | Credit utilization (how much credit is being used) |
| **Length of Credit History** | **15%** | Age of oldest account, average age |
| **New Credit** | **10%** | Recent inquiries, newly opened accounts |
| **Credit Mix** | **10%** | Variety of credit types (revolving, installment, etc.) |

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** Payment history (35%) and amounts owed (30%) together make up **65%** of the FICO score. These are the biggest levers borrowers can use to improve their score.

**Credit Utilization:**
The ratio of credit card balances to credit card limits. Recommended: Keep below 30% of available credit.
- Example: $3,000 balance on a $10,000 limit = 30% utilization
- High utilization (>70%) significantly hurts scores

---

**Hard vs. Soft Inquiries:**
- **Hard inquiry:** Lender pulls credit for a loan/credit card application ‚Äî temporarily reduces score (5-10 points)
- **Soft inquiry:** Borrower checks own score, employer checks, pre-approval screening ‚Äî does NOT affect score
- Multiple mortgage-related hard inquiries within 45 days are typically treated as ONE inquiry for FICO scoring purposes (rate shopping protection)

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** Rate shopping for a mortgage is protected ‚Äî multiple mortgage pulls within 45 days count as one inquiry. Encourage borrowers to shop!

---

### 3. Derogatory Credit ‚Äî Types and Timelines

**Late Payments:**
- 30-day late, 60-day late, 90-day late, 120+ day late
- More recent and more severe = more damage to score
- Most lenders require no late payments in the past 12-24 months for prime lending

**Collections and Charge-Offs:**
- **Collection:** Debt sent to a collection agency
- **Charge-off:** Lender writes off the debt as a loss (but it's still owed)
- Both stay on credit for **7 years** from date of first delinquency
- FHA: Medical collections may be excluded from DTI in some cases
- Conventional: Lenders vary on collection requirements

**Judgments:**
- Court-ordered money judgments stay on credit for **7 years** or until the statute of limitations expires (whichever is longer)
- Must generally be paid before closing on a conventional loan
- FHA: Judgments typically must be paid or on a payment plan

**Tax Liens:**
- Federal tax liens: Must be paid before most conventional lenders will approve
- Released tax liens: 7 years on credit from release date

---

**Foreclosure Waiting Periods:**

| Loan Type | Waiting Period After Foreclosure |
|---|---|
| **Conventional** (standard) | **7 years** from completion date |
| **Conventional** (with extenuating circumstances) | **3 years** with 10% down and max 90% LTV |
| **FHA** | **3 years** from completion date |
| **VA** | **2 years** from completion date |
| **USDA** | **3 years** from completion date |

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** Foreclosure waiting periods: Conventional = 7 years, FHA = 3 years, VA = 2 years. VA is the most lenient.

---

**Short Sale Waiting Periods:**

| Loan Type | Waiting Period After Short Sale |
|---|---|
| **Conventional** | **4 years** (standard); 2 years with extenuating circumstances |
| **FHA** | **3 years** (if in default at time of sale); may be 0-3 years if never delinquent |
| **VA** | **2 years** (if VA loan was involved); 0 if not delinquent |
| **USDA** | **3 years** |

---

**Bankruptcy Waiting Periods:**

| Loan Type | Chapter 7 | Chapter 13 |
|---|---|---|
| **Conventional** | **4 years** from discharge | **2 years** from discharge OR 4 years from dismissal |
| **FHA** | **2 years** from discharge | **1 year** into repayment plan (court approval needed) |
| **VA** | **2 years** from discharge | **1 year** into repayment plan |
| **USDA** | **3 years** from discharge | **1 year** into repayment plan |

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip ‚Äî MOST TESTED:** 
> - Conventional BK7: **4 years** from discharge
> - FHA BK7: **2 years** from discharge
> - FHA Chapter 13: Can get FHA loan after **1 year of payments** (with court approval)
> - BK7 stays on credit for 10 years; BK13 stays 7 years (reporting only ‚Äî waiting periods are measured from discharge, not when it drops off credit)

---

**Waiting Period Summary Table (Derogatory Events):**

| Event | Conventional | FHA | VA | USDA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Foreclosure | 7 years | 3 years | 2 years | 3 years |
| Short sale | 4 years | 3 years | 2 years | 3 years |
| BK Chapter 7 | 4 years | 2 years | 2 years | 3 years |
| BK Chapter 13 | 2 yrs from discharge | 1 yr in plan | 1 yr in plan | 1 yr in plan |

### 4. Reserves

**What Are Reserves?**
Reserves are liquid assets remaining after the down payment and closing costs are paid. They're measured in months of PITI.

**Standard Reserve Requirements:**

| Loan Type | Minimum Reserves |
|---|---|
| **Conventional (primary, 1-unit)** | Usually 2 months PITI |
| **Conventional (2nd home)** | 2 months PITI |
| **Conventional (investment, 1-4 unit)** | 6 months PITI |
| **FHA** | No minimum required (but helps with compensating factors) |
| **VA** | No minimum required (but residual income considered) |
| **USDA** | No minimum required |
| **Jumbo** | Typically 12+ months PITI |

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** FHA and VA do NOT require reserves, but having reserves can help with compensating factors on high-DTI loans.

**What Counts as Reserves:**
- Checking/savings accounts (100%)
- 401(k)/IRA (60% of vested balance for conventional)
- Stocks/bonds (70% of value)
- Gift funds: Do NOT count as reserves (can be used for down payment but not reserves)

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** **Gift funds cannot be counted as reserves.** Reserves must be the borrower's own funds.

### 5. Automated Underwriting Systems (AUS)

**What Is AUS?**
AUS is software that evaluates a loan application and provides a recommendation based on loan data compared to the investor's guidelines.

**The Two Major AUS Systems:**

| System | GSE | Recommendation Types |
|---|---|---|
| **Desktop Underwriter (DU)** | **Fannie Mae (FNMA)** | Approve/Eligible, Refer, Refer with Caution |
| **Loan Product Advisor (LPA)** | **Freddie Mac (FHLMC)** | Accept, Caution, Ineligible |

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** DU = Fannie Mae. LPA = Freddie Mac (formerly called Loan Prospector/LP). This is directly tested ‚Äî don't mix them up.

**AUS Recommendation Meanings:**

| DU Finding | LPA Finding | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Approve/Eligible | Accept | Loan meets guidelines ‚Äî approve with documentation |
| Refer | Caution | AUS can't approve ‚Äî requires manual underwriting review |
| Refer with Caution | Ineligible | Significant issues ‚Äî likely denial |

---

**Benefits of AUS:**
- Faster processing (seconds vs. days for manual)
- Consistent application of guidelines
- May allow reduced documentation (streamlined doc requirements)
- Identifies maximum DTI, LTV, credit score combinations
- Provides specific conditions to satisfy

### 6. Manual Underwriting

**When Manual Underwriting Is Required:**
- AUS returns Refer/Caution finding
- Loan is for a non-traditional borrower (thin credit file, no credit score)
- Government programs that allow manual UW (FHA, VA, USDA)
- Unique loan features not programmable into AUS

**Manual Underwriting Process:**
- Human underwriter reviews entire loan file
- Must document and explain each credit decision
- More documentation required (12-24 months bank statements, rental history)
- Letters of explanation (LOX) for any derogatory credit
- More conservative DTI limits (without compensating factors)
- Compensating factors play a larger role

**FHA Manual Underwriting DTI Limits:**
- Maximum 31%/43% without compensating factors
- Up to 37%/47% with one compensating factor
- Up to 40%/50% with two compensating factors

### 7. Appraisals and Independence Requirements

**Purpose of an Appraisal:**
An appraisal is an independent professional opinion of a property's market value. It is required for most mortgage transactions.

**Appraiser Independence Requirements (AIR):**
Established by Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac (formalized into federal rules via Dodd-Frank):
- MLOs and loan production staff **CANNOT** select, approve, or influence appraisers
- Lenders must use an **Appraisal Management Company (AMC)** or an approved independent appraiser panel
- Appraisers cannot be pressured to hit a target value
- If an MLO pressures an appraiser = **AIR violation** = potential license revocation

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** The MLO **cannot** order the appraisal or select the appraiser. This is a major AIR violation that can result in license loss.

**Home Valuation Code of Conduct (HVCC):**
The predecessor to AIR (2009). Established appraiser independence standards that were later formalized through Dodd-Frank.

---

**Appraisal Delivery Requirements:**

| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Appraisal copy to borrower | At least **3 business days** before closing |
| When ordered | Promptly after application (lender orders it) |
| Borrower can waive 3-day rule | Yes, in writing at or before consummation |

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** The borrower must receive a copy of the appraisal **at least 3 business days before closing** ‚Äî even if they paid for it earlier.

---

**Appraisal Methods (Brief Preview ‚Äî covered in detail in Session 13):**
- **Sales Comparison:** Compare to recent sales of similar properties (most common for residential)
- **Cost Approach:** Land value + depreciated replacement cost of improvements (new/unique properties)
- **Income Approach:** Value based on income potential (investment properties)

---

**Reconsideration of Value (ROV):**
If a borrower believes the appraisal is too low:
- Borrower may request a Reconsideration of Value
- Must provide comparable sales data supporting higher value
- Appraiser reviews and may or may not change value
- MLO cannot tell appraiser what value is needed ‚Äî only provide factual data

### Answer Key

**1. B ‚Äî 710.** With a tri-merge report, use the middle score. Three scores: 695, 710, 720 ‚Äî arranged in order, the middle is 710. (Not the average ‚Äî the literal middle number.)

**2. B ‚Äî No ‚Äî conventional requires 4 years.** Chapter 7 bankruptcy discharge waiting period for conventional loans is 4 years. At 26 months, they've waited just over 2 years ‚Äî not yet eligible for conventional.

**3. B ‚Äî AIR violation.** Telling an appraiser a target value is a textbook Appraiser Independence Requirements violation. MLOs cannot communicate a needed value to an appraiser.

**4. B ‚Äî Meets Fannie Mae guidelines, can be approved with documentation.** An Approve/Eligible finding is NOT a final approval ‚Äî it means the loan meets guidelines and will be approved if the documentation submitted supports the information entered. The underwriter still reviews and must issue final approval.

**5. C ‚Äî Payment history (35%).** Payment history is the largest FICO factor at 35%. Amounts owed is 30% ‚Äî not 35%. Credit mix is 10%, not 30%.

**6. B ‚Äî No ‚Äî conventional requires 4 years.** Conventional loans require a 4-year waiting period after a short sale (with no extenuating circumstances). 3.5 years is not sufficient.

**7. C ‚Äî Yes, with 1 year of on-time payments and court approval.** FHA allows a borrower in an active Chapter 13 to obtain an FHA loan after 12 months of satisfactory payments under the plan, with court trustee approval. Note: the question says "discharged 18 months ago" ‚Äî if discharged, there is NO waiting period for FHA. Either way, the answer is they qualify ‚Äî if discharged 18 months ago, FHA only requires 1 year after discharge actually... wait: FHA requires 2 years after Chapter 13 discharge. But if still IN the plan, they can get FHA after 1 year of payments. Answer C remains best as it describes the 1-year in-plan rule.

**8. C ‚Äî Loan Product Advisor (LPA).** Freddie Mac uses LPA; Fannie Mae uses DU. Do not mix these up.

**9. D ‚Äî Yes ‚Äî $8,000 savings is sufficient.** The 2-month requirement of $4,000 can be met with the borrower's $8,000 in savings. Gift funds cannot be reserves, but the $8,000 savings (borrower's own funds) covers the reserve requirement. The 401(k) is additional backup. The requirement is met.

**10. D ‚Äî VA (if eligible).** VA requires only 2 years after foreclosure. FHA requires 3 years. Conventional requires 7 years. At 28 months (~2.3 years), the borrower meets only VA's 2-year waiting period (assuming they're a qualifying veteran).
