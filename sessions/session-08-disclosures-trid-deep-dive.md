## üìñ PART 1: TOPIC DEEP DIVE

### Why This Session Matters

TRID (TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure) rules govern every consumer mortgage transaction. The exam tests specific timelines, tolerance categories, and business day definitions repeatedly. Expect 12-18 questions on this material ‚Äî it's one of the highest-yield topics on the exam.

---

### 1. What Is TRID?

**TRID = TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure**

Before TRID (effective October 3, 2015):
- Borrowers received a **Good Faith Estimate (GFE)** and a **Truth-in-Lending disclosure** at application
- Borrowers received a **HUD-1 Settlement Statement** at closing

After TRID:
- GFE + TIL disclosure ‚Üí replaced by **Loan Estimate (LE)**
- HUD-1 ‚Üí replaced by **Closing Disclosure (CD)**

**Why TRID was created:**
- Simplify disclosures
- Make it easier for borrowers to compare loan offers
- Give borrowers time to review before closing
- Ensure consistency between what was promised and what was delivered

**What TRID Covers:**
- Most closed-end consumer credit transactions secured by real property
- Includes: Purchase loans, refinances, construction-to-perm
- Does NOT cover: HELOCs, reverse mortgages, mobile homes not attached to land, commercial loans

### 2. The Complete TRID Timeline

Master this timeline ‚Äî it's tested constantly:

```
STEP 1: Application received (all 6 triggers met)
         ‚Üì
         3 BUSINESS DAYS
         ‚Üì
STEP 2: Loan Estimate (LE) delivered
         ‚Üì
         WAIT: Borrower has 10 business days to indicate intent to proceed
         (7-business-day waiting period begins from LE delivery)
         ‚Üì
STEP 3: 7-BUSINESS-DAY WAITING PERIOD
         (Cannot close until at least 7 business days after LE delivery)
         ‚Üì
STEP 4: Changed circumstances may trigger REVISED LE
         If revised LE issued: New 3-business-day waiting period before closing
         ‚Üì
STEP 5: Closing Disclosure (CD) delivered
         ‚Üì
         3 BUSINESS DAYS
         ‚Üì
STEP 6: Closing / Consummation
```

---

**Critical TRID Timelines:**

| Event | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Loan Estimate delivery | Within **3 business days** of complete application |
| Loan Estimate expiration | 10 business days after delivery |
| Waiting period after LE | **7 business days** must pass before closing |
| Closing Disclosure delivery | At least **3 business days** before closing |
| Revised CD waiting period | Certain changes = new 3-business-day waiting period |

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip ‚Äî The Key Numbers:** 3-7-3. **3** days to send LE, **7** days minimum before closing, **3** days before closing to send CD.

### 3. Business Day Definitions Under TRID

This is one of the trickiest parts ‚Äî there are TWO different definitions of "business day" used in TRID:

**Definition 1 ‚Äî "General Business Day":**
Used for: The 3-business-day rule for sending the LE after application

= Any day the creditor's offices are open to the public for carrying out substantially all business functions

In practice: Usually Monday through Friday, excluding federal holidays (if the lender is open on Saturday, Saturday counts)

---

**Definition 2 ‚Äî "Specific Business Day":**
Used for: The 7-business-day waiting period AND the 3-business-day CD delivery before closing

= All calendar days EXCEPT Sundays and federal public holidays

This means: **Saturdays count** as business days for these waiting periods!

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip ‚Äî Most Tested Distinction:** For the 7-day waiting period after the LE, and the 3-day CD review period before closing, **Saturdays DO count** as business days. Sundays and federal holidays do NOT.

**Federal Holidays (don't count for either definition):**
New Year's Day, Martin Luther King Jr. Day, Presidents Day, Memorial Day, Juneteenth, Independence Day, Labor Day, Columbus Day, Veterans Day, Thanksgiving, Christmas

---

**Practical Example:**
LE delivered on Wednesday.
- 7 business days (Specific): Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday (count) ‚Üí skip Sunday ‚Üí Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday ‚Üí Earliest closing = **Thursday** (8th day, or consumer can close on Wednesday if that's day 7)

> Wait ‚Äî let's be precise: If LE delivered Wednesday, the 7-day count begins the NEXT business day.
> Day 1 = Thursday, Day 2 = Friday, Day 3 = Saturday, Day 4 = Monday, Day 5 = Tuesday, Day 6 = Wednesday, Day 7 = Thursday (next). Earliest closing = **following Thursday**.

### 4. The Loan Estimate (LE) ‚Äî Page by Page

The LE is a 3-page document that must be given to the consumer within 3 business days of application.

**Page 1 ‚Äî Loan Terms and Projected Payments:**
- Loan terms: amount, interest rate, monthly P&I payment
- Can the interest rate increase? (Yes/No ‚Äî important for ARMs)
- Does the loan have a prepayment penalty? Balloon payment?
- Projected payments table: Shows payment breakdown over time (P&I, MIP/PMI, taxes, insurance)
- Estimated closing costs (total)
- Cash to close estimate

**Page 2 ‚Äî Closing Cost Details:**
- Section A: Origination charges (zero tolerance)
- Section B: Services borrower cannot shop for (zero tolerance)
- Section C: Services borrower CAN shop for (10% tolerance)
- Section E: Taxes and government fees
- Section F: Prepaids (insurance, interest, taxes)
- Section G: Initial escrow payment at closing
- Section H: Other costs
- Section I: Total closing costs

**Page 3 ‚Äî Comparisons and Contact Information:**
- APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
- Total interest percentage (TIP) ‚Äî total interest you'll pay over life of loan as % of loan
- In 5 years: Principal paid + total payments
- Lender/broker contact information
- Loan terms summary

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** Page 3 shows the **APR** and **TIP (Total Interest Percentage)**. These are the comparison tools. The APR is always higher than the interest rate because it includes fees.

---

### 5. The Closing Disclosure (CD) ‚Äî What's Inside

The CD is a 5-page document that must be given to the consumer at least 3 business days before closing.

**Page 1:** Loan terms, projected payments, closing costs summary (mirrors LE for comparison)
**Page 2:** Closing cost details (exactly mirrors LE Page 2 for comparison)
**Page 3:** Alternative cash to close table, summaries of transactions (purchase contract settlement)
**Page 4:** Additional information ‚Äî loan disclosures (escrow account, demand feature, etc.)
**Page 5:** Loan calculations (total payments, finance charge, APR, TIP), contact information, signature

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** The CD has **5 pages**; the LE has **3 pages**. The CD mirrors the LE so borrowers can compare what was promised to what's actually being charged.

---

**When Can a CD Be Issued Early?**
The lender may issue the CD earlier than 3 days before closing, as long as the 3-business-day minimum is met. Some lenders issue it 7-10 days early as a best practice.

**Events That Require a New 3-Day CD Waiting Period:**
- APR increases by more than 0.125%
- Loan product changes (e.g., fixed to ARM)
- Prepayment penalty is added

Events that do NOT require a new 3-day waiting period:
- APR decreases
- Seller concessions change
- Minor corrections

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** Not all CD changes restart the clock. Only APR increase > 0.125%, product change, or prepayment penalty addition restart the 3-day waiting period.

---

### 6. Changed Circumstances ‚Äî When Can You Issue a Revised LE?

A lender can issue a **revised LE** (and reset fee baselines) only when there is a valid "changed circumstance":

**Valid Changed Circumstances:**
1. **Natural disaster or emergency** affecting the property or the lender
2. **New information about the borrower or transaction** not previously known (e.g., appraisal reveals property issues, borrower reveals additional debt)
3. **Borrower-requested changes** (different loan amount, product, property)
4. **Interest rate lock** ‚Äî if rate wasn't locked at LE, once locked, lender may issue revised LE
5. **Expiration of a quoted rate** ‚Äî if borrower doesn't lock within 10 days of LE

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** A lender CANNOT issue a revised LE simply because they made an error in their initial estimate (if the error was within their control). Changed circumstances must be genuine.

**What Cannot Trigger a Revised LE:**
- Lender error in estimating fees
- Lender's own internal changes not related to borrower/property
- Market fluctuations in controllable costs

---

### 7. Fee Tolerance Categories

This is one of the most tested topics. Fees fall into three tolerance buckets:

**Category 1 ‚Äî ZERO TOLERANCE (Cannot increase at all):**
- Lender-imposed origination charges (points, origination fees)
- Transfer taxes
- Services where borrower is NOT allowed to shop (lender-required, lender-selected):
  - Appraisal fee (if lender selects)
  - Credit report fee
  - Flood determination fee
  - Upfront MIP or VA funding fee

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** Zero tolerance means exactly that ‚Äî if the fee was $500 on the LE, it MUST be $500 or less at closing. Any increase (even $1) is a tolerance violation.

**Category 2 ‚Äî 10% TOLERANCE (Can increase up to 10% total):**
- Recording fees (government fees)
- Services where borrower CAN shop but chose a lender-provided vendor from the Written List of Service Providers

**Category 3 ‚Äî UNLIMITED TOLERANCE (No cap on increases):**
- Prepaid interest (depends on closing date)
- Homeowner's insurance (borrower chooses policy)
- Initial escrow payments (property taxes ‚Äî amounts fluctuate)
- Services borrower shopped for themselves (chose their own vendor NOT on lender list)
- Owner's title insurance (if borrower opts in)

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip ‚Äî Tolerance Categories Summary:**
> - ZERO: Lender's own fees, required third-party services where lender picks vendor
> - 10%: Recording fees + services where borrower used lender's list
> - UNLIMITED: Prepaids, escrow, borrower-selected services

---

**What Happens When Tolerance Is Exceeded?**
The lender must provide a **cure** ‚Äî refund the difference to the borrower within 60 calendar days of consummation. This must come from the lender, not the settlement agent.

### 8. Affiliated Business Arrangements (AfBA)

**What Is an AfBA?**
When a settlement service provider refers business to an affiliated company in which the referrer has a financial interest.

**Examples:**
- Real estate company refers to its affiliated title company
- Mortgage company refers to its affiliated homeowner's insurance company

**RESPA Requirements for AfBA:**
1. The referring party must give the borrower an **AfBA Disclosure** at the time of referral
2. The disclosure must state the referring party's ownership interest
3. The disclosure must tell the borrower they are NOT required to use the affiliate

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** AfBA disclosure must be given at the **time of referral** ‚Äî not at closing, not at application, but AT THE MOMENT OF REFERRAL.

---

### 9. Servicing Disclosure

Under RESPA, lenders must provide a **Servicing Disclosure Statement** within **3 business days** of application.

This disclosure tells the borrower:
- Whether the lender intends to transfer servicing of the loan
- The percentage of loans the lender transferred in the past
- The borrower's rights when servicing is transferred

**When Servicing Is Actually Transferred:**
- 15 days before transfer: Notice from current servicer
- 15 days after transfer: Notice from new servicer
- 60-day grace period: During which borrower cannot be penalized for sending payment to old servicer

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** Servicing disclosure = within **3 business days** of application. Actual transfer notice = **15 days** before.

---

### 10. Right of Rescission

The right of rescission is the borrower's right to cancel a transaction within a specific period.

**When It Applies:**
- Refinances on a **primary residence**
- Home equity loans and HELOCs on a primary residence

**When It Does NOT Apply:**
- Purchase transactions (buying a home ‚Äî no rescission)
- Loans on investment properties or second homes
- Business-purpose loans

**Timeframe:**
- Borrower has **3 business days** to rescind (cancel) after consummation
- Runs through midnight of the 3rd business day
- If rescission notices weren't properly provided ‚Üí right extends to **3 years**

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** The right of rescission does NOT apply to purchase transactions. It only applies to refinances and HE loans on a primary residence. This is a classic exam trap.

**Rescission Business Day Definition:**
Same as TRID CD rule ‚Äî all days except Sundays and federal holidays. So Saturday counts.

### Answer Key

**1. C ‚Äî Thursday.** Application received Monday. 3 business days (using general business day definition, Mon-Sat): Day 1 = Tuesday, Day 2 = Wednesday, Day 3 = Thursday. LE due Thursday.

**2. C ‚Äî The following Thursday.** LE delivered Tuesday. 7-business-day waiting period (specific definition: all days except Sundays and federal holidays): Day 1=Wed, Day 2=Thu, Day 3=Fri, Day 4=Sat, skip Sun, Day 5=Mon, Day 6=Tue, Day 7=Wed. Can close Thursday (the day after the 7th day). Earliest close = **Thursday**.

**3. B ‚Äî Zero tolerance violation.** Credit report fees are in the zero tolerance category (required service where the lender selects the provider). Any increase above the LE amount is a violation.

**4. B ‚Äî APR increases from 6.875% to 7.025%.** The increase is 0.15%, which exceeds the 0.125% threshold, triggering a new 3-day waiting period. Seller concessions and minor decreases do not restart the clock.

**5. C ‚Äî Saturday night at midnight.** Right of rescission = 3 business days after closing using the specific business day definition (all days except Sundays and federal holidays). Closing Wednesday: Day 1=Thursday, Day 2=Friday, Day 3=Saturday. Expires Saturday midnight.

**6. B ‚Äî AfBA disclosure at the time of referral.** RESPA requires an AfBA disclosure to be given at the moment of referral ‚Äî not later.

**7. C ‚Äî APR and Total Interest Percentage (TIP).** Page 3 contains the comparison tools ‚Äî APR (cost of credit on an annual basis) and TIP (total interest as a percentage of loan over the life of the loan).

**8. B ‚Äî Yes ‚Äî a rate lock is a valid changed circumstance.** If the rate was not locked at the time of the initial LE and the borrower subsequently locks the rate, the lender may issue a revised LE reflecting the locked rate.

**9. C ‚Äî Days the lender's offices are open for business.** The 3-business-day rule for delivering the LE uses the "general business day" definition ‚Äî any day the creditor's offices are open for business (including some Saturdays if the lender operates them). This is different from the "specific business day" definition used for the 7-day waiting period.

**10. C ‚Äî Refund within 60 calendar days.** When a tolerance is violated (charged more than allowed), the lender must cure the violation by refunding the excess amount to the borrower within 60 calendar days of consummation.
