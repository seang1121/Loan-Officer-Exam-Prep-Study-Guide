### Why Ethics & SAFE Act Dominate the Exam

Ethics is the **second largest domain** on the NMLS exam (18% = ~21 questions). The SAFE Act is woven throughout Domain 1 (Federal Laws) and Domain 2 (State Content). Together, these topics account for nearly **30% of your score**. Know them cold.

---

### 1. The SAFE Act (Secure and Fair Enforcement for Mortgage Licensing Act ‚Äî 2008)

**Why it was passed:** The 2008 financial crisis exposed a Wild West of mortgage origination ‚Äî unlicensed, unaccountable MLOs selling toxic products. The SAFE Act created national standards.

**What it created:**
- **NMLS** ‚Äî Nationwide Multistate Licensing System & Registry
- A uniform framework requiring ALL MLOs to either register or be licensed
- National background checks, credit checks, education requirements
- A public database where consumers can look up any MLO

**The Core Distinction: Registration vs. Licensing**

| | Registered MLO | Licensed MLO |
|---|---|---|
| **Who** | Employees of federally regulated depository institutions (banks, credit unions, savings associations) | Employees of non-bank lenders, mortgage brokers, independent companies |
| **Process** | Register with NMLS ‚Äî employer sponsors registration | Apply for state license through NMLS |
| **Pre-licensing Ed** | Not required | 20 hours required |
| **SAFE Exam** | Not required | Must pass (75%+) |
| **State license** | Not required | Required (each state separately) |
| **Annual CE** | Not required | 8 hours required |
| **Background check** | Required | Required |

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** This distinction comes up constantly. Bank employees REGISTER. Brokers/independent MLOs get LICENSED. Know the difference cold.

### 2. SAFE Act Licensing Requirements ‚Äî Details

**Pre-Licensing Education (20 hours):**
- 3 hours: Federal mortgage law
- 3 hours: Ethics (fraud, consumer protection, fair lending)
- 2 hours: Non-traditional mortgage products
- 12 hours: Electives

**The National SAFE Mortgage Licensing Test:**
- Must score **75% or higher** to pass
- Two components: National + State-specific content (some states require separate state test)
- If you fail 3 times: must wait **6 months** before retaking

**Annual Continuing Education (8 hours):**
- 3 hours: Federal mortgage law
- 2 hours: Ethics
- 2 hours: Non-traditional mortgage products
- 1 hour: Elective
- Must be **NMLS-approved** courses
- **Prohibition:** Same course cannot be repeated in consecutive years for CE credit

**Background Check Requirements:**
- FBI criminal background check
- Credit history review
- Regulatory action review (prior licensing problems)

**Automatic Disqualifiers (no license possible):**
- ANY felony involving fraud, dishonesty, breach of trust, or money laundering ‚Äî no time limit (permanent bar)
- Felony drug conviction within the **past 7 years**
- Note: A 10+ year old drug felony is NOT an automatic bar, but states can still deny on fitness grounds

**Financial Responsibility Requirements:**
- MLOs must demonstrate financial responsibility (no history of financial negligence/irresponsibility)
- Some states require a minimum **net worth** (e.g., $25,000 for mortgage companies)
- Most states require a **surety bond** ‚Äî protects consumers from MLO misconduct (NOT the lender)
- Bond amount varies by state and loan volume (typically $25,000‚Äì$75,000+)

### 3. NMLS ‚Äî The Registry

**What NMLS tracks on every MLO:**
- Unique **NMLS ID number** (follows you for life ‚Äî doesn't change with employers)
- Employment history
- License status in all states
- Criminal history
- Regulatory/disciplinary actions
- Civil lawsuits related to mortgage activity
- Bankruptcy and financial disclosures

**Required Self-Reporting (within 30 days of the event):**
- Criminal convictions (any)
- Civil lawsuits related to financial services
- Regulatory actions in any state
- Bankruptcy, foreclosure, or certain financial events
- Change of employer

**License Status Types:**
| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Active** | Valid ‚Äî MLO can originate loans |
| **Inactive** | Not currently sponsored; cannot originate |
| **Pending** | Application submitted; not yet approved |
| **Suspended** | Temporarily revoked ‚Äî investigation or compliance issue |
| **Revoked** | Permanently removed; may reapply after conditions met |
| **Surrendered** | Voluntarily given up; treated like revocation in future applications |

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** When an MLO changes employers and the new employer hasn't accepted NMLS sponsorship yet ‚Üí license goes **inactive**. They cannot originate during that period.

### 4. Disciplinary Actions ‚Äî What Gets MLOs in Trouble

**License Suspension vs. Revocation:**
- **Suspension** = temporary; MLO may resume after corrective action
- **Revocation** = permanent removal (may reapply after waiting period in some states)
- **Voluntary surrender** = treated similarly to revocation in future applications

**Common Grounds for Disciplinary Action:**
- Making a false statement on a loan application
- Failing to disclose required information
- Steering borrowers to unsuitable loan products
- Accepting kickbacks (RESPA ¬ß8)
- Misusing borrower funds
- Failing to complete CE requirements
- Failing to disclose material events to NMLS within 30 days

**Penalties:**
- Civil fines (up to $25,000 per violation in many states)
- Mandatory CE (remediation)
- Probationary conditions
- Suspension or revocation
- Criminal referral for fraud

### 5. The Core Prohibited Acts ‚Äî Know These Cold

| Prohibited Act | Definition | Law Violated |
|---|---|---|
| **Steering** | Directing borrower to a loan that benefits the MLO at the borrower's expense (e.g., higher commission product when better product is available) | Dodd-Frank / LO Comp Rule |
| **Redlining** | Refusing to lend in geographic areas based on race/ethnic composition | Fair Housing Act, ECOA |
| **Reverse redlining** | Targeting minority communities with predatory, high-cost loan products | Fair Housing Act, ECOA |
| **Bait & switch** | Advertising favorable terms, then substituting worse terms at closing | UDAAP / Reg Z |
| **Kickbacks** | Paying or accepting fees for loan referrals with no service rendered | RESPA ¬ß8 |
| **Misrepresentation** | Falsifying or omitting material facts on a loan application or to a lender | 18 U.S.C. ¬ß 1014 |
| **Churning** | Repeatedly refinancing a borrower to generate fees without net benefit | State laws, UDAAP |
| **Occupancy fraud** | Falsely stating property will be owner-occupied when it's an investment property | 18 U.S.C. ¬ß 1014 |
| **Income fraud** | Inflating stated income to qualify a borrower | 18 U.S.C. ¬ß 1014 |
| **Ghost MLO fraud** | Processing loans under another MLO's NMLS number without their knowledge | SAFE Act, state law |
| **Loan stacking** | Submitting the same application to multiple lenders without disclosure | Mortgage fraud |
| **Appraisal pressure** | Asking or pressuring an appraiser to "hit the number" | AIR policy, FIRREA |

### 6. Fair Lending Laws ‚Äî The Two Main Frameworks

**Fair Housing Act (FHA) ‚Äî 7 Protected Classes:**
1. Race
2. Color
3. National Origin
4. Religion
5. Sex
6. Familial Status (families with children under 18, pregnant women)
7. Disability (Handicap)

**Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) ‚Äî Additional Protected Classes:**
- Age (must be of legal contracting age)
- Marital status
- Receipt of public assistance income (e.g., Social Security, alimony, child support)
- Good faith exercise of rights under the Consumer Protection Act

> ‚ö° **Exam Tip:** Fair Housing = 7 classes. ECOA adds age, marital status, public assistance income. Both apply to mortgage lending. Know which class is which law.

**Two Types of Discrimination:**

| Type | Definition | Intent Required? |
|---|---|---|
| **Disparate Treatment** | Different treatment of an applicant based on a protected class characteristic | Yes ‚Äî intentional |
| **Disparate Impact** | A neutral policy that disproportionately harms a protected class | No ‚Äî effect-based |

**Examples:**
- Offering different rates to borrowers of different races with identical profiles = Disparate Treatment
- A minimum loan amount policy that statistically results in significantly fewer approvals in minority neighborhoods = Disparate Impact

### 7. MLO Advertising Ethics

**Required in ALL MLO advertising:**
- Your **NMLS ID number**
- Your **company name** (as registered with NMLS)
- State licensing information if required by state

**TILA Regulation Z ‚Äî Advertising Trigger Terms:**
Advertising ANY of the following triggers FULL rate disclosures:
- A specific interest rate ("as low as 5.99%")
- A specific dollar amount of payment
- A specific number of payments
- The amount of any down payment

If you trigger, you must also disclose: APR, loan term, payment amount, down payment.

**Cannot do in advertising:**
- Use "guaranteed" rate claims unless actually guaranteed
- Imply government affiliation or endorsement (e.g., "Government Mortgage Relief Program")
- Omit required NMLS/company info
- Use the word "free" for anything that has a cost
- Advertise FHA/VA rates without proper disclosures

---

### 8. Financial Responsibility & Continuing Obligations

**MLOs must maintain throughout licensure:**
- Good credit standing (major derogatory events must be reported)
- Up-to-date NMLS record (within 30 days of any material change)
- Completed annual CE before renewal deadline
- Surety bond (if required by state)
- Active sponsorship by a licensed employer

**If any of these lapse:**
- License can be placed in inactive status, suspended, or revoked
- MLO cannot originate until the issue is resolved and NMLS is updated

## üÉè PART 2: FLASHCARDS

**Q: What does SAFE stand for?**
A: Secure and Fair Enforcement for Mortgage Licensing Act (2008)

**Q: What does NMLS stand for and what does it do?**
A: Nationwide Multistate Licensing System & Registry ‚Äî tracks all MLO licenses, background info, disciplinary actions, and employment history nationwide.

**Q: How many pre-licensing education hours are required?**
A: 20 hours (3 federal law, 3 ethics, 2 non-traditional products, 12 electives)

**Q: How many CE hours are required annually?**
A: 8 hours (3 federal law, 2 ethics, 2 non-traditional products, 1 elective)

**Q: What passing score is required on the SAFE exam?**
A: 75% or higher

**Q: If you fail the SAFE exam 3 times, what happens?**
A: You must wait 6 months before taking it again.

**Q: Bank employee MLOs must ___ with NMLS. Non-bank MLOs must ___.**
A: Register | Be state-licensed

**Q: What is the permanent bar under the SAFE Act regarding felonies?**
A: Any felony involving fraud, dishonesty, breach of trust, or money laundering is a permanent disqualifier ‚Äî regardless of when it occurred.

**Q: What is the time-limited SAFE Act disqualifier for drug felonies?**
A: A drug-related felony within the past 7 years automatically disqualifies an applicant.

**Q: Within how many days must an MLO report a criminal conviction to NMLS?**
A: 30 days

**Q: What does a surety bond protect?**
A: Consumers harmed by the MLO's conduct ‚Äî not the lender, not the MLO.

**Q: What is "steering"?**
A: Directing a borrower to a loan product that benefits the MLO rather than the borrower.

**Q: What is "redlining"?**
A: Refusing to make loans in specific geographic areas based on the racial/ethnic composition of those areas.

**Q: What is "reverse redlining"?**
A: Targeting minority communities with predatory or unfavorable loan products.

**Q: Name the 7 protected classes under the Fair Housing Act.**
A: Race, Color, National Origin, Religion, Sex, Familial Status, Disability.

**Q: What additional classes does ECOA protect beyond the Fair Housing Act?**
A: Age, Marital Status, Receipt of Public Assistance Income, Exercise of Consumer Protection rights.

**Q: What is disparate treatment vs. disparate impact?**
A: Disparate treatment = intentional different treatment based on protected class. Disparate impact = neutral policy that disproportionately harms a protected class (no intent required).

**Q: What does an MLO's NMLS ID number do?**
A: It follows the MLO for life ‚Äî regardless of employer changes ‚Äî and is the unique identifier in the national registry.

**Q: When does an MLO's license become "inactive"?**
A: When their employer sponsorship is not active in NMLS (e.g., during an employer transition).

**Q: What is "churning"?**
A: Repeatedly refinancing a borrower to generate fees without providing a net financial benefit to the borrower.

**Q: What is occupancy fraud?**
A: Falsely claiming a property will be used as a primary residence when it is actually an investment property.

**Q: What must ALL MLO advertisements include?**
A: The MLO's NMLS ID number and their company's name as registered.

**Q: If an MLO is named in a civil lawsuit related to mortgage activity, what must they do?**
A: Amend their NMLS record within 30 days to disclose the lawsuit.

**Q: What is "ghost MLO fraud"?**
A: Processing loans under another MLO's NMLS number without their knowledge ‚Äî to circumvent licensing requirements or spread production volume.

**Q: Can an MLO use the same CE course two years in a row?**
A: No ‚Äî NMLS prohibits completing the same approved course in consecutive years for CE credit.

## üéØ PART 4: SCENARIO PRACTICE

*Apply your knowledge to these 5 real-world situations.*

---

**Scenario A**
An MLO notices that their lender's automated underwriting system is returning lower scores for applicants in a predominantly Hispanic ZIP code ‚Äî even when income, credit, and LTV are comparable to applicants in other areas. The MLO should:

**Best action:** Report the pattern to compliance/management for a fair lending analysis. This is a potential disparate impact concern that could expose the company to regulatory scrutiny. Staying silent makes the MLO complicit.

---

**Scenario B**
An MLO discovers during processing that the borrower signed the application but didn't disclose a pending $45,000 IRS tax lien. The title search reveals it. What now?

**Best action:** The lien must be disclosed to the lender. If the lien is material (which $45,000 almost certainly is), the underwriter will need to determine its impact on the loan. The MLO cannot conceal it. Omitting material information from a lender is fraud.

---

**Scenario C**
A real estate agent texts an MLO: "Send me 10 of those branded market update reports and I'll make sure all my buyers use you this year." How should the MLO respond?

**Best action:** Decline. This is a proposed quid pro quo referral arrangement ‚Äî the agent will send referrals in exchange for marketing materials. Even without cash changing hands, this implicates RESPA ¬ß8. The MLO can maintain a business relationship but cannot condition it on referral promises.

---

**Scenario D**
An MLO is completing the annual CE and realizes they already took the exact same "Federal Mortgage Law" NMLS course last year from the same provider. Can they use it again this year?

**Best action:** No. NMLS prohibits an MLO from taking the same approved course in consecutive years for CE credit. The MLO must select a different approved course to satisfy the requirement.

---

**Scenario E**
An MLO is approached by a developer who says "I'll pay your company $500 for every one of my buyers you approve." The MLO's company manager likes the idea. What should the MLO do?

**Best action:** Refuse and escalate to compliance. Paying or receiving per-approval/per-transaction fees for referrals violates RESPA ¬ß8 regardless of who initiates the arrangement. The MLO has personal liability for participating, even at a manager's direction.
