### 1. FHA Loans â€” Federal Housing Administration

**History & Purpose:**
The FHA was created in 1934 during the Great Depression to increase homeownership by making low-down-payment mortgages available. FHA doesn't lend money â€” it **insures** loans made by approved lenders.

**FHA Loan Basics:**

| Feature | FHA Rule |
|---|---|
| **Minimum down payment** | 3.5% (credit score 580+) |
| **Minimum down payment** | 10% (credit score 500-579) |
| **Credit score minimum** | 500 (below 500 = ineligible) |
| **Loan limits** | Vary by area (set annually by FHA based on area median home prices) |
| **Debt-to-income** | Up to 43% standard; up to 57% with strong compensating factors |
| **Property type** | Primary residence only |
| **Income limits** | None (FHA has no income limits) |
| **Assumable** | Yes â€” can be assumed by a qualified buyer |
| **Manual underwriting** | Available when AUS can't approve |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** FHA has **no income limits** â€” anyone can use FHA regardless of income. USDA has income limits. This distinction is tested.

---

**FHA Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP):**

FHA loans require two types of mortgage insurance:

| Type | Amount | When Paid |
|---|---|---|
| **Upfront MIP (UFMIP)** | **1.75%** of base loan amount | At closing (can be financed into loan) |
| **Annual MIP** | Typically **0.55%** (varies by LTV, term) | Monthly, added to payment |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** FHA UFMIP = **1.75%**. This is one of the most commonly tested numbers on the exam. Memorize it.

**Annual MIP Rates (approximate for 30-year, standard loan):**
- LTV > 95%: 0.55% annually
- LTV 90-95%: 0.50% annually
- LTV â‰¤ 90%: 0.50% annually

**When Does FHA MIP End?**

This is a critical, commonly tested distinction:

| Down Payment | When MIP Ends |
|---|---|
| **Less than 10%** | MIP lasts for the **life of the loan** |
| **10% or more** | MIP cancelled after **11 years** |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** If the borrower puts less than 10% down on an FHA loan, they pay MIP **forever** (unless they refinance out of FHA). This is a major disadvantage compared to conventional loans where PMI cancels at 78% LTV automatically.

---

**FHA Loan Limits:**
- "Floor" (low-cost areas): 65% of conforming loan limit
- "Ceiling" (high-cost areas): 150% of conforming loan limit
- Alaska, Hawaii, Guam, U.S. Virgin Islands: Even higher limits
- Published annually by HUD

---

**FHA Key Characteristics:**
- **Assumable:** FHA loans can be assumed by a qualified buyer â€” lender approval required
- **Streamline Refinance:** FHA-to-FHA refinance with reduced documentation (no new appraisal in many cases)
- **Secondary Financing:** Allows down payment assistance programs as secondary financing if they meet FHA guidelines
- **Property Requirements:** Properties must meet HUD's Minimum Property Standards (MPS)

---

**FHA vs. Conventional â€” When Does FHA Make Sense?**

FHA is better when:
- Credit score is below 620 (can't qualify conventional)
- Down payment is limited (3.5% vs. 5% conventional minimum)
- Higher DTI needed

Conventional is better when:
- Credit score 740+ (PMI is cheaper, rate is better)
- Putting 20%+ down (avoid MIP entirely)
- Can afford higher down payment to avoid lifetime MIP

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** With less than 10% down, FHA MIP lasts forever. Conventional PMI cancels at 78% LTV. For borrowers with decent credit who can make a meaningful down payment, conventional often wins long-term.

### 2. VA Loans â€” Department of Veterans Affairs

**Who Is Eligible:**
- Active duty service members
- Veterans (with qualifying service)
- National Guard and Reserve members (with qualifying service)
- Surviving spouses of service members killed in action (in some cases)

**VA Loan Basics:**

| Feature | VA Rule |
|---|---|
| **Minimum down payment** | **0%** (no down payment required) |
| **Credit score minimum** | No VA minimum (lenders typically require 620) |
| **Loan limits** | No limit for full entitlement (since 2020 Blue Water Navy Act) |
| **PMI** | **None required** |
| **Property type** | Primary residence only |
| **Income limits** | None |
| **Assumable** | Yes â€” can be assumed by veterans AND civilians |
| **Certificate of Eligibility (COE)** | Required to prove VA entitlement |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** VA loans have **no down payment requirement** and **no PMI** â€” these are the two biggest advantages. The VA does NOT set a minimum credit score (though lenders do).

---

**VA Funding Fee:**

Instead of MIP/PMI, VA loans have a one-time **funding fee** that goes to the VA to sustain the program.

| Category | Down Payment | First Use | Subsequent Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Active/Veteran | 0% | **2.15%** | **3.30%** |
| Active/Veteran | 5-9.99% | 1.50% | 1.50% |
| Active/Veteran | 10%+ | **1.25%** | 1.25% |
| Reserves/Guard | 0% | 2.40% | 3.30% |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** The range is **1.25% to 3.30%**. First use with 0% down = 2.15%. Subsequent use with 0% down = 3.30%. Funding fee can be financed into the loan.

**Exemptions from VA Funding Fee:**
- Veterans receiving disability compensation (10%+ disability rating)
- Purple Heart recipients (on active duty)
- Surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from service-connected disability

---

**VA Entitlement:**

VA entitlement is the amount the VA guarantees on a loan. Understanding entitlement is critical.

**Basic Entitlement:** $36,000 (or 25% of the conforming loan limit in some cases)

**Bonus/Second-Tier Entitlement:** Additional entitlement available when basic is used or for higher-cost loans
- Total entitlement = 25% of the conforming loan limit

**Full Entitlement (post-2020):**
- Veterans with full entitlement have NO loan limit
- Can buy any price home with $0 down and no loan limit
- Full entitlement exists when: No current VA loan, OR previous VA loan was paid off and entitlement restored

**Reduced Entitlement:**
- If a veteran still has an active VA loan, remaining entitlement is reduced
- Veteran may still use remaining entitlement but may need a down payment

**Restoring Entitlement:**
1. Pay off and sell the property (sell and restore)
2. Have an eligible veteran assume the loan
3. One-time restoration: VA allows one restoration even if property still owned

---

**VA IRRRL â€” Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (Streamline):**
- VA-to-VA refinance with reduced documentation
- No new Certificate of Eligibility typically needed
- No appraisal required (in most cases)
- Must result in lower rate (with some exceptions for ARM-to-fixed)
- Small funding fee (0.5%)

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** IRRRL stands for **Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan** â€” often called the "VA streamline" or "VA IRRRL" (pronounced "Earl"). It's VA-to-VA only.

---

**VA Residual Income:**
Unlike FHA and conventional which focus on DTI, VA underwriting emphasizes **residual income** â€” the amount of money left over after all monthly obligations.

Minimum residual income varies by:
- Number of family members
- Geographic region (Northeast, Midwest, South, West)
- Loan amount

This is what allows VA loans to be approved with higher DTIs when residual income is strong.

### 3. USDA Loans â€” U.S. Department of Agriculture

**Purpose:**
USDA loans (Rural Development Guaranteed Loan Program) are designed to promote homeownership in rural and certain suburban areas.

**USDA Loan Basics:**

| Feature | USDA Rule |
|---|---|
| **Minimum down payment** | **0%** |
| **Credit score** | Typically 640+ for GUS approval; manual UW available below |
| **Income limits** | **115% of Area Median Income (AMI)** â€” income limits DO apply |
| **Property location** | Must be in a **USDA-eligible rural area** |
| **Property type** | Primary residence only |
| **Loan limits** | No formal limit (but income-based repayment capacity limits practical maximum) |
| **Assumable** | Yes, with lender approval |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** USDA has **income limits at 115% of AMI**. This is the critical fact that distinguishes USDA â€” you can earn too MUCH to qualify. This is unique; FHA and VA have no income limits.

---

**USDA Guarantee Fees:**

| Fee | Amount |
|---|---|
| **Upfront Guarantee Fee** | **1.00%** of base loan amount (can be financed) |
| **Annual Fee** | **0.35%** of outstanding balance annually |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** USDA upfront = **1%**. Annual = **0.35%**. Compare to FHA: 1.75% upfront, ~0.55% annual. USDA is significantly cheaper on mortgage insurance.

---

**USDA Property Eligibility:**
- Must be in a USDA-designated rural area (checked at usda.gov)
- Many suburban areas outside major metro areas qualify
- Single-family homes, condos, new construction can qualify
- Property must be in good repair (no major structural issues)
- USDA has its own property standards

**GUS â€” Guaranteed Underwriting System:**
USDA's version of AUS. Submitting to GUS is required. GUS issues:
- Accept â€” loan approved through GUS
- Refer â€” requires manual underwriting
- Refer with Caution â€” significant issues

### 5. FHA vs. Conventional with PMI â€” Which Is Better?

**The Break-Even Question:**

FHA advantages:
- Lower credit score accepted
- Lower down payment
- Easier qualifying with higher DTI

Conventional advantages:
- PMI cancels at 78% LTV automatically (or 80% LTV on request)
- No lifetime mortgage insurance requirement
- Often cheaper for borrowers with 680+ credit scores

**Key Rule of Thumb:**
If a borrower with 620-639 credit score and 3-5% down payment can qualify for either:
- FHA: MIP forever (with <10% down)
- Conventional: PMI at ~0.8-1.2% annually, but PMI cancels at 78% LTV

For borrowers who plan to stay long-term, **conventional is often cheaper** once PMI cancels.

### Answer Key

**1. C â€” 10%.** FHA requires a minimum 10% down payment when the credit score is between 500-579. For scores 580 and above, the minimum is 3.5%. A score of 560 falls in the 500-579 range.

**2. C â€” 2.15%.** For a first-time use with 0% down, a regular veteran (not Reserves/Guard) pays a VA funding fee of 2.15%.

**3. D â€” Never â€” life of the loan.** With a 5% down payment on an FHA loan, the LTV at origination exceeds 90%, meaning MIP will last for the full loan term. MIP only has an 11-year cancellation if the initial down payment was 10% or more.

**4. B â€” Family with $140,000 income over 115% AMI limit.** USDA income limits are at 115% of Area Median Income. If the area's limit is $125,000 and the family earns $140,000, they exceed the income limit and are ineligible.

**5. B â€” Apply for restoration of entitlement.** When a veteran pays off a VA loan and sells the property, they need to formally apply to the VA to have their entitlement restored before using it again.

**6. B â€” PMI will cancel at 78% LTV.** Conventional PMI cancels when LTV reaches 78% (automatically), while FHA MIP with <10% down lasts forever. **7. D â€” 0.35%.** USDA annual guarantee fee is 0.35% of the outstanding balance. The upfront fee is 1.00%.

**8. C â€” USDA.** Only USDA has geographic restrictions â€” properties must be in USDA-eligible rural or certain suburban areas. FHA and VA have no geographic restrictions.

**9. C â€” Residual income.** VA underwriting places primary emphasis on residual income (money left over after all debts), which is why VA allows higher DTIs when residual income is sufficient.

**10. B â€” 11 years.** With exactly 10% down, the FHA loan starts at 90% LTV. MIP cancels after 11 years (if LTV is at or below 90% at origination and the borrower put 10% or more down). This is the threshold â€” below 10% = life of loan; 10% or more = 11 years.
