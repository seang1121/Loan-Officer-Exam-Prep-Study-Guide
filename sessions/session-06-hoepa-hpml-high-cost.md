### The APOR â€” Average Prime Offer Rate

Before diving into HOEPA and HPML, you must understand **APOR**.

**What Is APOR?**
The Average Prime Offer Rate is the benchmark interest rate used to determine whether a loan is "high-cost" or "higher-priced."

- Published **weekly** by the CFPB (originally published by the Federal Reserve)
- Based on survey data from the Freddie Mac Primary Mortgage Market Survey
- Represents the average rate offered to well-qualified borrowers for comparable transactions
- A separate APOR exists for each loan type (fixed-rate 30-year, ARM 5/1, etc.)

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** APOR is published **weekly** by the CFPB. It's the baseline â€” both HOEPA and HPML are defined by how far a loan's APR exceeds APOR.

---

### 1. HOEPA â€” Home Ownership and Equity Protection Act

**Background:**
HOEPA was originally enacted in 1994 as an amendment to TILA (Truth in Lending Act) and was significantly expanded by Dodd-Frank in 2010. It targets the most predatory loans and is implemented under **Regulation Z**.

**HOEPA applies to:**
- Closed-end home purchase loans
- Refinance transactions
- Home equity loans
- Does NOT apply to: HELOCs, reverse mortgages (HECMs), construction loans

---

**HOEPA Triggers â€” When Does a Loan Become "High-Cost"?**

A loan becomes a **High-Cost Mortgage** under HOEPA if it meets ANY of these three triggers:

| Trigger | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **Rate trigger (1st lien)** | APR > APOR + **6.5%** |
| **Rate trigger (subordinate lien)** | APR > APOR + **8.5%** |
| **Fee trigger** | Points & fees > **5% of loan** OR **$1,000** (whichever is less, on loans under $20,000) |
| **Prepayment penalty trigger** | Prepayment penalty > 2% of amount prepaid, OR prepayment penalty can be charged more than 36 months after loan |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip â€” Critical Numbers:**
> - HOEPA 1st lien: **APR > APOR + 6.5%**
> - HOEPA subordinate lien: **APR > APOR + 8.5%**
> - Fee trigger: **5%** of loan amount

---

**HOEPA Restrictions â€” What High-Cost Loans CANNOT Have:**

| Restriction | Rule |
|---|---|
| **No balloon payments** | (except small creditors, qualified mortgages, bridge loans) |
| **No negative amortization** | Balance cannot grow |
| **No prepayment penalties** | Prohibited entirely after Dodd-Frank expansion |
| **No acceleration clauses** | Cannot call the loan due without cause |
| **No loan flipping** | Cannot refinance a HOEPA loan within 1 year without benefit to borrower |
| **No advance payments** | Cannot require more than 2 advance periodic payments at closing |
| **No lender-financed credit insurance** | Cannot wrap insurance premiums into the loan |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** After Dodd-Frank, **prepayment penalties are completely prohibited** on HOEPA (high-cost) loans. This is tested.

---

**HOEPA Required Disclosures:**

1. Must provide enhanced HOEPA disclosures **at least 3 business days before consummation** (closing)
2. Disclosure must state specific terms: APR, monthly payment, amount borrowed
3. Must include notice that borrower does NOT have to complete the loan
4. Borrower must receive **housing counseling** from a HUD-approved counselor BEFORE closing

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** HOEPA requires **mandatory housing counseling** before closing â€” this is unique to high-cost loans.

---

**HOEPA Enhanced Disclosures Include:**

- "You are not required to complete this agreement"
- "If you sign, you could lose your home and your money invested in it"
- "Interest rates and monthly payments can increase"
- The specific APR and monthly payment amounts

---

### 2. HPML â€” Higher-Priced Mortgage Loans

**Background:**
HPML was created by the Federal Reserve Board in 2008 (now administered by the CFPB) under **Regulation Z**. It catches loans that are higher-priced than prime but don't meet HOEPA's higher thresholds.

---

**HPML Triggers â€” When Is a Loan "Higher-Priced"?**

| Trigger | Threshold |
|---|---|
| **Rate trigger (1st lien)** | APR > APOR + **1.5%** |
| **Rate trigger (subordinate lien)** | APR > APOR + **3.5%** |
| **Rate trigger (jumbo)** | APR > APOR + **2.5%** (for jumbo loans) |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip â€” Critical Comparison:**
> - HPML 1st lien: **APR > APOR + 1.5%** (lower threshold, more loans caught)
> - HOEPA 1st lien: **APR > APOR + 6.5%** (much higher threshold, fewer loans)
> HPML has the LOWER threshold. More loans qualify as HPML than as HOEPA.

---

**HPML Requirements â€” What Higher-Priced Loans Must Have:**

| Requirement | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Mandatory escrow** | Must establish escrow for taxes and insurance for **at least 5 years** |
| **Appraisal required** | Must obtain a written appraisal (AMC model) |
| **Second appraisal** | May be required for certain flipped properties |
| **No prepayment penalties** | Generally prohibited; narrow exceptions allowed |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip:** **HPML requires a 5-year escrow account** â€” this is one of the most tested facts. The escrow for taxes and insurance must be maintained for a minimum of 5 years.

---

**HPML Appraisal Rules:**

For HPML loans, in addition to the standard appraisal:
- An additional **free copy of the appraisal** must be given to the borrower at least 3 business days before consummation
- For properties that were sold within 180 days at a lower price (potential flipping), a **second independent appraisal** may be required at NO COST to the borrower

---

**Exemptions from HPML:**

- Qualified Mortgages (standard QM â€” but see rebuttable presumption)
- HELOCs
- Reverse mortgages (HECMs)
- Construction loans (12 months or less)

---

### 3. Side-by-Side Comparison: HOEPA vs. HPML

This is the most important table in this session. Memorize it.

| Feature | HOEPA (High-Cost) | HPML (Higher-Priced) |
|---|---|---|
| **Also called** | High-Cost Mortgage | Higher-Priced Mortgage Loan |
| **1st lien rate trigger** | APR > APOR + **6.5%** | APR > APOR + **1.5%** |
| **Subordinate lien trigger** | APR > APOR + **8.5%** | APR > APOR + **3.5%** |
| **Fee trigger** | >5% of loan amount | None |
| **Escrow required** | No specific escrow rule | **5 years mandatory** |
| **Mandatory counseling** | **Yes â€” required** | No |
| **Appraisal required** | No specific rule | **Yes** |
| **Prepayment penalty** | **Prohibited entirely** | Generally prohibited |
| **Negative amortization** | **Prohibited** | No specific rule |
| **Balloon payment** | **Generally prohibited** | No specific rule |
| **Special disclosures** | Yes â€” 3 business days before closing | No (standard LE/CD apply) |
| **Threshold level** | HIGHER threshold (fewer loans) | LOWER threshold (more loans) |

> âš¡ **Exam Tip â€” The Key Distinction:** HOEPA has **higher** rate thresholds and **more** restrictions. HPML has **lower** thresholds and **fewer** (but still important) restrictions. If a loan is HOEPA, it is almost certainly also HPML (HOEPA is a subset of higher-priced loans).

---

### 4. Escrow Requirements in Detail

**Standard QM Loans:**
- No federal requirement to escrow â€” lender's choice
- If lender doesn't escrow, borrower pays taxes and insurance directly

**HPML Loans:**
- **Mandatory 5-year escrow** for property taxes and homeowner's insurance
- After 5 years, borrower may cancel escrow under certain conditions

**How to Remember:**
- Standard loan = "You're responsible for your own taxes and insurance (maybe)"
- HPML = "We're holding your tax/insurance money for at least 5 years"
- HOEPA = Even worse terms mean even more scrutiny, but the escrow rule comes from HPML threshold

---

### 5. How HOEPA and HPML Interact with QM

| Loan Type | QM Status | HPML Status | HOEPA Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prime 30-year fixed | Usually yes | No | No |
| Slightly above-prime rate | May be QM | Possibly (if >APOR+1.5%) | No |
| Well above prime | Non-QM | Yes (HPML) | Possibly if >6.5% above |
| Predatory/extreme rate | Non-QM | Yes | Yes (HOEPA) |

---

### 6. Practical Examples

**Example 1:**
Current APOR for 30-year fixed = 6.00%
Loan APR = 7.75%
Rate spread = 1.75% above APOR
- HPML? Yes (1.75% > 1.5% threshold for 1st lien)
- HOEPA? No (1.75% < 6.5% threshold)
- Requirements: 5-year escrow, appraisal required

**Example 2:**
Current APOR for 30-year fixed = 6.00%
Loan APR = 13.25%
Rate spread = 7.25% above APOR
- HPML? Yes (7.25% > 1.5%)
- HOEPA? Yes (7.25% > 6.5%)
- Requirements: ALL HOEPA restrictions + counseling + enhanced disclosures + 5-year escrow

**Example 3:**
Current APOR = 6.00%
Loan APR = 6.90%
Loan fees = 4.5% of loan amount
- HPML? No (0.90% < 1.5% threshold)
- HOEPA? No (rate is fine; fees are 4.5% < 5% threshold)
- Standard loan, no special requirements

### Key Numbers to Memorize

| Concept | Number |
|---|---|
| HOEPA 1st lien rate trigger | APOR + **6.5%** |
| HOEPA subordinate lien trigger | APOR + **8.5%** |
| HOEPA fee trigger | **5%** of loan amount |
| HPML 1st lien rate trigger | APOR + **1.5%** |
| HPML subordinate lien trigger | APOR + **3.5%** |
| HPML jumbo trigger | APOR + **2.5%** |

### Answer Key

**1. B â€” HPML only.** APR of 8.25% vs. APOR of 6.50% = spread of 1.75%. This exceeds the HPML 1st lien threshold of 1.5%, making it HPML. However, 1.75% is well below the HOEPA 1st lien threshold of 6.5%, so it is NOT HOEPA.

**2. B â€” HUD-approved housing counseling.** HOEPA requires mandatory counseling from a HUD-approved housing counselor before the borrower signs the loan documents.

**3. C â€” 5 years.** HPML requires a mandatory escrow account for property taxes and homeowner's insurance for at least 5 years.

**4. B â€” Weekly.** The CFPB publishes APOR on a weekly basis.

**5. C â€” Balloon payment.** Balloon payments are generally prohibited on HOEPA high-cost mortgages. Adjustable rates, points, and fixed rates are all permissible features.

**6. C â€” Both HPML and HOEPA.** A 7.5% spread above APOR exceeds both the HPML threshold (1.5%) and the HOEPA threshold (6.5%) for first liens, making it both HPML and HOEPA simultaneously.

**7. D â€” APR > APOR + 8.5%.** For subordinate liens, HOEPA's rate trigger is 8.5% above APOR (vs. 6.5% for first liens). For reference, HPML's subordinate lien trigger is 3.5%.

**8. C â€” A written appraisal.** HPML requires a written appraisal performed by a certified or licensed appraiser. The other items listed are HOEPA requirements (counseling, enhanced disclosures) not HPML-specific requirements.

**9. C â€” 30-year ARM at 14% when APOR is 7%.** This loan has a 7% spread above APOR, which exceeds the HOEPA 1st lien threshold of 6.5%. Note: HELOCs are exempt from HOEPA.

**10. B â€” At least 3 business days before closing.** HOEPA requires enhanced disclosures to be provided at least 3 business days before consummation, giving borrowers time to review and potentially walk away.
