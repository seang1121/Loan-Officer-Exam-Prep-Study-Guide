# Official NMLS SAFE MLO Test Content Outline

> Source: mortgage.nationwidelicensingsystem.org (official NMLS document)
> This is the exact blueprint the exam is built from. Every question maps to a topic below.

---

## Exam Structure
- **120 total questions** (115 scored + 5 unscored/experimental)
- **190 minutes** time limit
- **75% to pass** (87 out of 115 scored)
- **$110 fee**
- **58% first-time pass rate**

## I. FEDERAL MORTGAGE RELATED LAWS (24%)

### RESPA (Reg X)
- Origins and purpose; definition of "mortgage broker"
- Applicable loan types
- RESPA prohibitions, limitations & exemptions
- Settlement services
- Required borrower information on application
- Foreclosure process
- Initial escrow statements

### ECOA (Reg B)
- Permissible acts
- Factors that cannot be used to discriminate
- Circumstances where loan can be denied
- Notifying borrower of action taken (timing)
- Required disclosures when application denied
- Adverse action: definition/examples/notifications/timing
- Information required on application; definition of "elderly"
- MLO actions when borrower refuses to provide race/gender info
- Co-signer requirements
- Acceptable income for loan review
- Creditworthiness factors

### TILA (Reg Z)
- Purpose of TILA
- Loans covered
- Definitions: APR, finance charge, dwelling, residential mortgage loan
- Notice of right to rescind; refinance rescind scenarios
- HOEPA / high-cost mortgages (12 CFR 1026.32)
- Higher-priced mortgage loans (12 CFR 1026.35)
- MLO compensation (12 CFR 1026.36(d))

### TRID ("Know Before You Owe")
- Purpose of TRID
- Loans covered
- Loan Estimates: facts, required info, charges/fees
- Definition of "loan consummation"
- Special information booklet
- Closing Disclosure
- Disclosure timing
- MLO actions if TRID disclosure is incomplete
- "Change of circumstances"
- Borrower's right to rescission
- Annual escrow statement

### Other Federal Laws
- HMDA (Reg C)
- FCRA / FACTA
- FTC Red Flag Rules
- BSA / AML
- Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) — Privacy, Do-Not-Call
- Mortgage Acts and Practices — Advertising (Reg N)
- E-Sign Act
- USA PATRIOT Act
- Homeowners' Protection Act (PMI Cancellation Act)
- Dodd-Frank Act

### Regulatory Authority
- CFPB
- HUD

## II. UNIFORM STATE CONTENT (11%)

### SAFE Act & CSBS/AARMR Model State Law
- General purpose and scope
- Documents filed for public record

### State Mortgage Regulatory Agencies
- Regulatory powers and responsibilities
- NMLS Registry and relationship with state regulators
- Frequency of exams
- MLO unique identifiers
- CFPB authority and LO rule (dual compensation)

### License Law and Regulation
- People required to be licensed
- MLO-licensed services
- Allowable activities by underwriters, clerical staff, loan processors
- Entities requiring licensed MLO
- Businesses NOT required to be licensed (depository institutions)
- Licensee qualifications & application process:
  - Pre-license education
  - Background checks
  - Felony charges
- Waiting period for test retakes
- Sponsorship requirement
- Definition of "MLO"
- Grounds for denying a license
- License maintenance: renewal, CE requirements, active status
- Retaking the exam if license goes inactive

### NMLS Requirements
- Change of employment notifications
- Required submissions/disclosures
- NMLS identifier requirements
- Temporary Authority to Originate

### Compliance
- State regulator authority to examine records and interview employees
- Prohibited acts:
  - Paying for real estate agent ads
  - Using supervisor's NMLS ID
  - Omitting debt not on credit report
  - Bait-and-switch
- Required conduct: providing documents in complaint investigations, record retention
- Advertising: NMLS ID required in all advertisements

## III. GENERAL MORTGAGE KNOWLEDGE (20%)

### Qualified and Non-Qualified Mortgage Programs
- Qualified mortgages
- Conventional/conforming (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac)
- Government (FHA, VA, USDA)
- Conventional/nonconforming: jumbo, Alt-A, subprime
- Non-qualified mortgage

### Mortgage Loan Products
- Fixed-rate mortgages
- ARMs
- Purchase money second mortgages
- Balloon mortgages
- Reverse mortgages (HECM)
- HELOC
- Construction mortgages
- Interest-only mortgages

### Industry Terms
- Loan terms: subordinate loans, escrow, lien, tolerances, rate lock, table funding
- Disclosure terms: yield spread premiums, servicing transfers, lender credits
- Financial terms: discount points, 2-1 buy-down, LTV, accrued interest, finance charges, daily simple interest
- General terms: subordination, conveyance, primary/secondary market, assumable loan, APR

## IV. MORTGAGE LOAN ORIGINATION ACTIVITIES (27%)

### Loan Inquiry & Application
- Loan inquiry process — required disclosures
- Accepting applications
- Offering/negotiating terms
- Managing information
- Permissible questions
- Gift donors
- Verification: authorization forms, bank asset percentages, verifying employment
- Suitability of products
- Accuracy/tolerances: zero tolerance, 10% tolerance
- Disclosure timing: Know Before You Owe, notification of action, early disclosures, AfBA
- Loan Estimate timing: initial, revised, expiration, tolerance corrections
- Closing Disclosure
- Homeownership Counseling Disclosure

### Qualification: Processing & Underwriting
- Borrower Analysis: assets, liabilities, income, credit report, qualifying ratios, ATR
- Appraisals: purpose, approaches (market/income/cost), timing, independence
- Title Report: obtaining, timing, preliminary reports
- Insurance: flood, PMI, hazard/homeowner, government mortgage insurance

### Closing
- Title and title insurance
- Settlement/Closing Agent: eligible signatures, power of attorney
- Explanation of fees: HUD-1, title insurance, pre-paids, escrow, origination fees
- Required closing documents
- Funding — rescission periods

### Financial Calculations
- Periodic interest
- Monthly payments
- Down payments
- Closing costs/prepaids
- ARM adjustments

---

## V. ETHICS (18%)

### Ethical Issues
- Prohibited acts: redlining, RESPA prohibitions, kickbacks/compensation
- Fairness in lending: referrals, coercion, appraiser conflicts, discrimination
- Fraud detection: asset/income/employment fraud, red flags, occupancy fraud
- Suspicious activity; verifying application info
- Advertising: misleading info, due diligence, "unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts"
- Predatory lending and steering

### Ethical Behavior in Loan Origination
- Financial responsibility: permitted fees/compensation, fee changes, referral fees, fee splitting
- Handling borrower complaints
- Mortgage company compliance: material information, employer info
- Relationships with consumers: personal info/cybersecurity, conflicts of interest, credit reports
- Unreported/fluctuating income, gifts/unexplained deposits, appraiser interactions
- Truth in marketing and advertising
- General business ethics: falsified info, solicited/unsolicited advice, outside parties seeking info
