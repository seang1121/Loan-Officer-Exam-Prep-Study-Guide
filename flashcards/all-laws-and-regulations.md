## INDIVIDUAL LAW FLASHCARDS

### SAFE Act

**Card 1**
Q: What does the SAFE Act stand for and when was it enacted?
A: **Secure and Fair Enforcement for Mortgage Licensing Act** — enacted in **2008**

**Card 2**
Q: What did the SAFE Act create?
A: The **NMLS** (National Mortgage Licensing System) — a centralized licensing and registration system for all MLOs

**Card 3**
Q: Under the SAFE Act, what is the difference between bank MLOs and non-bank MLOs?
A: Bank MLOs **register** with NMLS; non-bank MLOs must be **state-licensed** through NMLS

---

### TILA — Truth in Lending Act (Regulation Z)

**Card 4**
Q: What does TILA require lenders to disclose?
A: The **APR (Annual Percentage Rate)** — the true cost of credit including fees and interest expressed as an annual rate

**Card 5**
Q: What is Regulation Z?
A: The implementing regulation for TILA (Truth in Lending Act)

**Card 6**
Q: Under TILA, when does the right of rescission apply?
A: For refinances and home equity loans on a **primary residence** — NOT for purchase transactions

**Card 7**
Q: What is the TILA right of rescission period?
A: **3 business days** after consummation (all days except Sundays and federal holidays)

---

### RESPA — Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (Regulation X)

**Card 8**
Q: What does RESPA Section 8 prohibit?
A: **Kickbacks** — paying or receiving anything of value for referral of settlement service business

**Card 9**
Q: What does RESPA Section 9 prohibit?
A: Requiring buyers to use a specific **title company** as a condition of the sale

**Card 10**
Q: What does RESPA Section 10 govern?
A: **Escrow accounts** — limits on how much can be collected; annual escrow analysis required

**Card 11**
Q: What is the maximum escrow cushion a servicer can collect?
A: **2 months** of estimated tax and insurance payments

**Card 12**
Q: What is the penalty for a RESPA Section 8 kickback violation?
A: Up to **$10,000 fine and/or 1 year in prison** per violation

**Card 13**
Q: When was RESPA enacted?
A: **1974**

---

### ECOA — Equal Credit Opportunity Act (Regulation B)

**Card 14**
Q: What are ECOA's 9 protected classes?
A: Race, Color, Religion, National Origin, Sex, Marital Status, Age, Receipt of public assistance, Exercise of CCPA rights

**Card 15**
Q: What does ECOA require when credit is denied?
A: An **adverse action notice** within **30 days** with specific reasons for denial

**Card 16**
Q: Can a lender require a spousal signature under ECOA?
A: Only if the spouse is a co-borrower, on title, in a community property state, or their income is needed — NOT solely because of marriage

**Card 17**
Q: What regulation implements ECOA?
A: **Regulation B**

---

### Fair Housing Act

**Card 18**
Q: What are the 7 protected classes under the Fair Housing Act?
A: Race, Color, Religion, National Origin, Sex, Familial Status, Handicap/Disability

**Card 19**
Q: What are the exemptions to the Fair Housing Act?
A: (1) Private seller selling own single-family home without agent, (2) Owner-occupied buildings with 4 or fewer units (Mrs. Murphy), (3) Religious organizations for member housing, (4) Private clubs for member housing

**Card 20**
Q: What is blockbusting?
A: Inducing property owners to sell by representing that minority groups are moving into the neighborhood — a Fair Housing violation

**Card 21**
Q: Does the Fair Housing Act cover AGE as a protected class?
A: **No** — age is protected under ECOA but NOT the Fair Housing Act

---

### HMDA — Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (Regulation C)

**Card 22**
Q: What is the primary purpose of HMDA?
A: To detect and prevent **redlining** by requiring lenders to report where they make (and don't make) loans

**Card 23**
Q: What is the HMDA LAR?
A: Loan Application Register — the database of all mortgage applications a covered lender must maintain and submit annually

**Card 24**
Q: What is the HMDA LAR filing deadline?
A: **March 1** of the following year

**Card 25**
Q: What data is collected on the HMDA LAR?
A: Loan type, purpose, property location, loan amount, race/ethnicity/sex/age of applicant, action taken, income, credit score, DTI, LTV, pricing information

---

### CRA — Community Reinvestment Act

**Card 26**
Q: What does CRA require?
A: Federally regulated **depository institutions** (banks) to meet the credit needs of **low- and moderate-income (LMI)** communities in their assessment areas

**Card 27**
Q: Does CRA apply to mortgage companies?
A: **No** — CRA applies only to federally regulated depository institutions, not non-bank mortgage lenders

**Card 28**
Q: What are the four CRA examination categories?
A: Lending test, Investment test, Service test, and Community Development test

---

### FCRA — Fair Credit Reporting Act

**Card 29**
Q: What are the permissible purposes for pulling a credit report?
A: Credit application, employment (with consent), insurance, government licensing, court order, legitimate business need

**Card 30**
Q: How long do most negative items stay on a credit report?
A: **7 years** from the date of first delinquency

**Card 31**
Q: How long does a Chapter 7 bankruptcy stay on a credit report?
A: **10 years** from the filing/discharge date

**Card 32**
Q: How many days does a CRA have to investigate a credit dispute?
A: **30 days** (45 days if dispute submitted with annual free report)

---

### BSA — Bank Secrecy Act

**Card 33**
Q: What is a Currency Transaction Report (CTR)?
A: A report required when a financial institution handles cash transactions **exceeding $10,000** in a business day

**Card 34**
Q: What is a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)?
A: A report filed when a financial institution suspects a transaction involves illegal activity; filed within **30 days**

**Card 35**
Q: What is structuring?
A: Intentionally breaking up cash transactions to stay under the $10,000 CTR threshold — **illegal** even if the money is clean

**Card 36**
Q: What agency administers the BSA?
A: **FinCEN** — Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (Treasury Department)

---

### USA PATRIOT Act — CIP

**Card 37**
Q: What does CIP stand for?
A: **Customer Identification Program** — required under Section 326 of the PATRIOT Act

**Card 38**
Q: What are the 4 required CIP identifying elements?
A: **Name, Date of Birth, Address, Identification Number** (SSN or passport)

**Card 39**
Q: What list must institutions check before doing business with a customer?
A: The **OFAC SDN list** (Specially Designated Nationals) — cannot do business with sanctioned individuals

---

### Dodd-Frank Act

**Card 40**
Q: What year was Dodd-Frank enacted and what crisis prompted it?
A: **2010** — in response to the **2008 financial crisis**

**Card 41**
Q: What did Dodd-Frank create?
A: The **CFPB**, the ATR rule, the QM definition, the LO Compensation Rule, and TRID

**Card 42**
Q: What is the Volcker Rule (Dodd-Frank)?
A: Prohibits banks from **proprietary trading** (trading securities for the bank's own profit) and from owning hedge funds or private equity funds

---

### HOEPA — High-Cost Mortgages

**Card 43**
Q: What is the HOEPA rate trigger for a first lien?
A: APR exceeds APOR by more than **6.5%**

**Card 44**
Q: What special protection does HOEPA require before closing?
A: Mandatory **HUD-approved housing counseling** and enhanced disclosures at least **3 business days before** closing

**Card 45**
Q: Are prepayment penalties allowed on HOEPA loans?
A: **No** — completely prohibited on high-cost (HOEPA) loans post-Dodd-Frank

---

### HPML — Higher-Priced Mortgage Loans

**Card 46**
Q: What is the HPML rate trigger for a first lien?
A: APR exceeds APOR by more than **1.5%**

**Card 47**
Q: What is the mandatory escrow requirement for HPML?
A: Lenders must establish an escrow account for taxes and insurance for at least **5 years**

**Card 48**
Q: Is an appraisal required for an HPML?
A: **Yes** — a written appraisal by a certified/licensed appraiser is required

---

### TRID — TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosures

**Card 49**
Q: What did TRID replace?
A: The **Good Faith Estimate (GFE)** and Truth-in-Lending disclosure were replaced by the **Loan Estimate (LE)**; the **HUD-1** was replaced by the **Closing Disclosure (CD)**

**Card 50**
Q: What is the TRID 3-7-3 rule?
A: Send LE within **3** business days of application; minimum **7** business days between LE and closing; CD delivered at least **3** business days before closing

## Quick-Reference: Law to Regulation Mapping

| Federal Law | Implementing Regulation |
|---|---|
| TILA | **Regulation Z** |
| RESPA | **Regulation X** |
| ECOA | **Regulation B** |
| HMDA | **Regulation C** |
| CRA | Regulation BB |
| Dodd-Frank (LO Comp, ATR, QM) | **Regulation Z** |
| HOEPA | **Regulation Z** |
| HPML | **Regulation Z** |
| TRID | **Regulation X and Z** |

> ⚡ **Exam Tip:** Notice that TILA, HOEPA, HPML, Dodd-Frank ATR/QM, and LO Comp are ALL in **Regulation Z**. RESPA and TRID are in **Regulation X** (with TRID also in Regulation Z). ECOA = Reg B. HMDA = Reg C.
